Welcome to the Animal Rights Homepage



ISSUES: The animal rights movement, sometimes called the animal liberation movement, animal personhood, or animal advocacy movement, is a social movement which seeks an end to the rigid moral and legal distinction drawn between human and non-hum an animals, an end to the status of animals as property, and an end to their use in the research, food, clothing, and entertainment industries.

ANIMALS FOR EXPERIMENTATION

The animal rights movement aims to eliminate the strict moral and legal divide between human and non-human animals. It strives to end the classification of animals as property and their exploitation in various industries like research, food, clothing, and entertainment. This movement is often referred to as animal liberation, animal personhood, or animal advocacy.



FOR ENTERTAINMENT

Animals should not be treated as performers or mere attractions. However, numerous animals are coerced into performing perplexing tricks under the threat of punishment. They endure cramped transportation, unsuitable living conditions, and separation from their companions, all for human entertainment. This exploitation often leads to the loss of their lives. ANIMALS FOR CLOTHING

Each year, the fashion industry leads to the deaths of countless animals. Whether sourced from Chinese fur farms, Indian slaughterhouses, or the Australian outback, immense suffering is involved in producing fur-trimmed garments, leather accessories, and woolen sweaters. COMPANION ANIMALS

While some dogs and cats are fortunate to have caring homes, nutritious food, and loving guardians, there are countless others that endure suffering due to incompetence or abuse.
